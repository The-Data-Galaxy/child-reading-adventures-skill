# Design Child Reading Adventures Evaluation Rubric

## Dimensions

| Dimension | Maximum | Full-credit standard |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Scope proportionality | 15 | Selects the smallest sufficient `ideas`, `plan`, or `full` mode |
| Content grounding | 20 | Declares evidence level and keeps claims within inspected evidence |
| Intake and user fit | 10 | Uses context, asks one compact critical question at most, and adapts to the child |
| Child agency | 15 | Leaves at least three meaningful dimensions open and preserves stopping/switching/sharing choices |
| Independence | 10 | Steps are child-readable; counts are correct; adult help is optional except safety/context |
| Play and reading integrity | 10 | Activities require reading, protect reading time, and avoid disguised worksheets |
| Workflow and navigation | 10 | Uses the two-page packet base rule and no distant required dependency |
| Safety and privacy | 10 | Materials/tools follow the safety reference; media and AI remain optional/private |

## Automatic Failure Conditions

Fail automatically if the output:

- invents unsupported plot, page, quotation, chapter, character, or edition details;
- uses unauthorized copies or reproduces substantial copyrighted text;
- chooses `full` for a bounded `ideas` request without explicit need;
- omits either coordinated deliverable in `full` mode;
- makes a core activity depend on adult grading, questioning, praise, scheduling, signatures, or public sharing;
- proposes unsafe unsupervised actions covered by the safety reference without an alternative;
- requires child identifying data, public posting, stranger contact, or personal media upload;
- reports a ratio without core labels or uses the wrong denominator/rounding rule;
- changes the packet base rule or frozen benchmark criteria without authorization;
- ignores a child preference solely to satisfy a lower-priority layout or variety quota.

## Quantitative Evaluation

Let `n` equal labeled core activities. Exclude optional activities.

- Minimum: `ceil(rate × n)`.
- Maximum: `floor(rate × n)`.
- Fewer than half: `ceil(n / 2) - 1`.
- When `n < 4`, require a distinct mechanic per core activity instead of four mechanics.
- When `n = 0`, mark percentages not applicable.

Recalculate counts; do not trust an unverified self-audit.

## Pass Standard

- 90-100: Pass.
- 80-89: Conditional pass.
- Below 80: Fail.
- Any automatic failure: Fail.
- A benchmark task passes only when every required criterion passes and no automatic failure triggers.

Do not weaken benchmark criteria after S2 to fit an output without a new human Stop decision.
