# Safety and optional tools

Apply this reference when an activity uses loose parts, string, movement, camera/audio, online sharing, or AI. Preserve play and independence without treating risk as a test of courage.

## Materials and movement

- Offer a no-material or low-risk version whenever possible.
- Do not propose unsupervised heat, electricity, sharp tools, heights, roads, open water, unknown plants/animals/substances, or forceful throwing.
- Avoid choking-size loose parts for children who may mouth objects or when age/accessibility context is unknown.
- Keep string, cord, ribbon, and loops on a tabletop. Never place them around the neck or body, suspend weight from them, or use them where a child could become entangled.
- Use soft, light objects for target or movement play. Keep play away from faces, breakables, stairs, traffic, and other people who have not joined.
- When adult material preparation or safety help is genuinely needed, mark it `🟠 求助卡`, offer an independent alternative, and do not count the activity as `🟢 自主完成`.

## Photos, audio, and sharing

- Treat a photo or recording as optional; a private mark, object name, or no trace must remain valid.
- Default media to local/private use. Do not require a face, full name, school, uniform, address, location, contact details, or another child's identity.
- Do not require public posting, messaging strangers, joining a public group, or sending media to the designer, teacher, or platform.
- Ask before recording another person. Provide a solo/offline version when consent is absent.

## Optional AI

- Keep AI optional and provide a non-AI route that reaches the same reading goal.
- Do not ask the child to upload personal information, a face/voice recording, schoolwork containing identifiers, private messages, or location data.
- Use an adult-managed account or access boundary when the service requires one; this makes AI a `🔵 工具可选` or `🟠 求助卡`, never a core dependency.
- Do not use AI output or model memory as evidence for a book's plot, quotation, page, chapter, character, or edition. Apply `references/content-grounding.md` independently.
- Phrase AI results as suggestions that the child may ignore, change, or challenge. Do not let AI grade the child or decide the correct emotional response.

## Safety wording

Keep child-facing safety language short and concrete. Put detailed adult preparation notes in the adult-facing plan, not inside the play invitation. Block an unsafe request briefly and offer the nearest safe/private alternative.
