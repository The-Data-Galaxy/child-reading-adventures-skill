# Content-grounding policy

Book-specific activity design must be grounded in the exact work and, where relevant, the exact edition.

## Evidence levels

### Level A — Text-grounded

Available material includes the complete book or the complete portions to be used, supplied by the user or lawfully accessible. Activities may cite supported chapters, characters, events, structures, concepts, and short necessary excerpts. Check edition metadata.

### Level B — Section-grounded

Available material includes a verified table of contents plus reliable previews, official excerpts, sample chapters, or the relevant pages. Activities may be specific only to the portions actually examined. Do not extrapolate unseen sections.

### Level C — Bibliographic

Only edition metadata, publisher descriptions, catalog records, reviews, or general synopses are available. Confirm identity, genre, broad positioning, and likely reading structure. Produce only a provisional genre-level route, activity menu, and handbook shell. Do not claim knowledge of the book's detailed contents.

### Level D — Unverified

The title, author, edition, translation, abridgment, or contents cannot be reliably matched. Stop book-specific design and request identifying material.

## Source order

Prefer, in order:

1. user-supplied book files, pages, contents, and copyright page;
2. publisher or author materials and official authorized previews;
3. public library catalogs and lawful digital lending platforms;
4. reliable bibliographic databases and reputable bookseller metadata;
5. secondary reviews only for orientation, never as sole support for detailed activities.

Do not use unauthorized scans, download links, copied full texts, anonymous summaries, homework-answer sites, or model memory as content evidence. A lawful lending page confirms availability, not necessarily detailed contents.

## Search workflow

1. Match title, author/translator, publisher, publication year, ISBN, edition, and abridged/full status.
2. Locate the table of contents and authorized preview or relevant text.
3. Record what was actually inspected.
4. Assign an evidence level for each title.
5. Constrain every activity to supported material.
6. State what additional material would unlock more specific design.

## Page-reference gate

Treat pagination as edition-specific evidence, not a work-level fact.

- Use an exact source-book page number or range only when the matching edition's pages were actually inspected.
- Do not transfer page numbers across editions, translations, abridgments, printings, ebooks, audiobooks, previews, or other formats.
- If the text or event is verified but matching pagination is not, omit every source-page number, range, blank, and fill-in field. Use a verified chapter, section heading, or event cue instead.
- If no accurate content is available, do not create a book-specific lookup cue. Use a generic reading choice or request identifying material.
- Internal handbook page numbers may still be used for navigation; label them clearly so they cannot be mistaken for source-book pages.
- Keep the limitation and request for better material in the adult plan. Never ask the child to solve the mismatch.

## Copyright boundary

Use retrieved text to understand structure and design original activities. Do not reproduce substantial copyrighted text, create a replacement edition, or include unnecessary quotations in the handbook.
